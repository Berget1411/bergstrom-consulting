## Design Context

### Users
B2B clients — enterprises and scale-ups evaluating a consulting partner for legal tech, commerce, AI, and product work. They arrive with a specific problem, often under time pressure. They scan fast. They're not looking for inspiration; they're evaluating whether to reach out. Secondary: industry peers and potential collaborators.

**Context of use**: Desktop-primary, likely during working hours, in a professional setting. Dark forced theme appropriate — reduces distraction, signals focus.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp. Technical. Minimal.**

Not warm — serious. Not flashy — precise. The interface should feel like a well-written brief: nothing wasted, every word placed. Credibility is established through restraint, not decoration.

Emotional goal: within 5 seconds, a potential client thinks "this person is serious." Not "this looks cool." Serious.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark, forced. Not for style — for focus.
- **Color**: Monochromatic dark base with a single warm orange accent (`oklch(0.7 0.2 45)`). Rare use of accent = high signal. Don't dilute it.
- **Typography**: Bebas Neue (display) + IBM Plex Sans (body) + IBM Plex Mono (labels). These are kept by choice — the pairing works for the editorial + technical register. Future refinements can explore less-common alternatives, but the current stack is intentional.
- **Zero border-radius**: Hard edges throughout. Deliberate.
- **Motion**: GSAP scroll-driven interactions. Motion serves orientation and transitions, not spectacle. Staggered reveals, scroll parallax, scramble text — all purposeful.
- **Anti-references**: Generic agency sites (hero → services → testimonials → CTA). No gradient blobs. No startup energy.

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the argument.** Every element that isn't there strengthens what is. Default to removal, not addition.

2. **Credibility through precision.** Spacing, alignment, and hierarchy are the signals. A 1px border at the right place outweighs any decorative flourish.

3. **Accent is a scarce resource.** Orange appears only where it must: interactive states, key labels, moments of emphasis. Overuse kills it.

4. **Motion as orientation, not performance.** Animations tell the user where they are in the flow. If a motion doesn't communicate state or guide attention, cut it.

5. **The work is the content.** Supporting elements (nav, labels, colophon) recede. Case studies lead. Never let chrome compete with substance.
